About Me

I am a senior Computer Science student at Ursinus College in Pennsylvania. Being at a small school, I've gotten involved with a little bit of everything, from being elected President of our ACM chapter, to playing violin and viola in the orchestra, to helping create the Ursinian Affiliate of the Alpha Delta Phi Society

When I'm not putting together our annual Hackathon or spending hours in the lab tweaking my projects, I'm still usually found with my head buried in my computer playing League of Legends or reading through my ever-growing collection of sci-fi, fantasy novels, and comics.

While I enjoy exploring all aspects of the tech world, my passion is in using software to integrate modern tools into the classroom. From spending two summers teaching at a technology camp for kids, to my research with Dr. April Kontostathis on the role apps can play in the classroom, I love figuring out innovative ways to utilize technology.
